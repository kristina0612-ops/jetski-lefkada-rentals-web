Nobrainer AI · Website Improvement Proposal

Make your website
as exceptional as your journeys.

Dream Escapes already speaks beautifully to a premium traveler — the brand, the tagline, the promise of the extraordinary. A few focused refinements would help that promise convert more visitors into inquiries.

Below are six concrete ideas, ranked roughly by impact. Nothing here requires a redesign — most are surgical changes that protect what you already have.

Six small shifts, real results.

🎯

1. A hero that points somewhere

The hero ends on a beautiful tagline, but offers the visitor no next step. The eye lands, admires — and scrolls away.

Suggested Add one clear primary button in the hero: "Plan Your Escape" or "Browse Villas" — pointing to your strongest section.
🍪

2. A lighter cookie consent

The current consent banner is large and covers the most valuable real estate on the page — the first impression. Premium visitors decide in seconds.

Suggested Switch to a slimmer banner (bottom strip, single accept + settings link) that leaves the hero fully visible.
💬

3. A visible inquiry path on every section

Right now there is no recurring call-to-action between sections. Once a visitor scrolls past the menu, they have no obvious way back to "talk to us".

Suggested A small, recurring "Get in touch" button or sticky WhatsApp link — present but never intrusive.
⚡

4. Faster mobile experience

Most luxury-travel research starts on a phone. Image weight, font loading, and unused scripts are the usual culprits — and all fixable without touching the design.

Suggested Compress hero imagery, defer non-critical scripts, and load fonts strategically. Visitors stay longer.
👤

5. Show the people behind the brand

Premium travel is bought on trust. Visitors want to know who is crafting their journey before they share their dates and budget.

Suggested A short "Meet your travel curator" section — a real photo, two or three sentences, one line of credentials.
🧭

6. Stronger separation of services

Hotel & Villa Collections and Car & Boat Rental currently compete in the same visual lane. Each deserves its own clear entry point.

Suggested Two distinct service tiles directly under the hero — one for Stay, one for Move. The visitor self-selects instantly.

This is a starting point — not a finished proposal. Every point above can be scoped, prioritized, or skipped depending on what matters most for the coming season. Happy to walk through it together.
